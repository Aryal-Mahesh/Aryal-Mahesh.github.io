I am a PhD candidate in [Computational Soft Matter Physics](https://www.ndsu.edu/physics/) at North Dakota State University, working with [Prof. Alan R. Denton](https://www.ndsu.edu/pubweb/~adenton/). My research combines statistical mechanics, Monte Carlo simulations, and molecular dynamics to study how macromolecular crowding and confinement shape the behavior of polymers and biopolymers — questions with direct relevance to intracellular environments, drug delivery, and biosensing.

In recent work, I extended Flory–Rehner theory to a ternary mixture of polymer networks and nanoparticles, providing a framework for nanoparticle partitioning in polymer gels. I am now extending this framework to biopolymers in flexible nanoconfinement, with the goal of building tunable models for folding and unfolding in crowded media.

Before NDSU, I earned an M.Sc. in Solid State Physics from Tribhuvan University in Nepal, where my thesis used atomistic MD simulations to study the downhill-folding protein 2CYU. During the COVID-19 pandemic I worked on an interdisciplinary project combining molecular docking and MD to screen drug candidates against SARS-CoV-2 RdRp, and contributed to a curated phytochemical database of medicinal plants from Bagmati Province, Nepal.

I am broadly interested in computational biophysics — building physics-based, scalable simulation workflows for biologically realistic systems. Feel free to get in touch.
